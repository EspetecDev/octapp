  <action>
    Prerequisite: Plan 02 Task 2 is complete. The groom device has unlocked the chapter and the sensor minigame screen should already be active on GROOM.

    Step 1 — On GROOM (Android phone):
    1. Confirm the groom screen is showing the sensor minigame interface — you should see a tilt meter (fill bar or needle graphic) and a "Enable Sensor" or "Tap to start" button, plus a countdown timer (30s)
    2. If the groom page is NOT on the sensor minigame (e.g., it's still on waiting screen), check that the chapter was unlocked in Plan 02 Task 2 before proceeding

    Step 2 — On GROOM (Android phone):
    1. Tap the "Enable Sensor" (or "Start") button on the sensor minigame screen
    2. Observe: on Android, NO operating system permission dialog should appear (this is expected — Android auto-grants DeviceMotion access)
    3. The tilt meter should immediately become active — the fill bar or needle should be visible

    Step 3 — Physical tilt test:
    1. Hold the Android phone upright in portrait orientation
    2. Slowly tilt the phone to the RIGHT (clockwise when viewed from the front)
    3. Observe the tilt meter on screen — the fill bar should increase as you tilt right
    4. Tilt back to upright — the fill bar should decrease
    5. Tilt to the LEFT — the fill bar should decrease further (or behave as expected for left tilt)
    6. Confirm the meter is responding dynamically to physical movement — not frozen or static

    Step 4 — Complete or time out the minigame:
    Option A (win): Tilt the phone aggressively to the right (normalized >= 0.8 — roughly 45+ degrees from vertical) and hold it there until the win overlay appears
    Option B (let it time out): Hold the phone upright and wait 30 seconds for the countdown to expire; the loss overlay will appear
    Either outcome is acceptable — what matters is the overlay appears and the meter responded during play.

    Step 5 — Observe any browser console errors:
    If the tilt meter did NOT respond, connect the Android phone to the PC via USB, open chrome://inspect in the PC browser, find the remote tab, and open DevTools to check for JavaScript exceptions.
  </action>
  <acceptance_criteria>
    - Tapping "Enable Sensor" on GROOM (Android) does NOT show an OS permission dialog (expected behavior on Android — per D-07)
    - After tapping "Enable Sensor", the tilt meter is visibly active (not frozen or stuck at zero)
    - Physically tilting the phone RIGHT causes the tilt meter fill bar to increase (observable change on screen)
    - Physically tilting the phone back to upright causes the fill bar to decrease
    - A win overlay OR loss overlay appears when the minigame ends (either via win condition or 30s timeout)
    - No JavaScript exceptions in browser console during the sensor test
    Result to record: Android Sensor Test PASS or FAIL (if FAIL, note whether meter was frozen, whether dialog appeared unexpectedly, or whether an overlay never appeared)
  </acceptance_criteria>
